About

It took 3 years for me to get to this point.

I first started writing on Medium, left it to drift into one of my ideas-never-fully-realised black holes. I then took to Goodreads, initiated a very ambitious 18-book challenge, but ended up with zero entries. On some days, I found it hard to sleep because my mind was running through ideas I had from the day, and how I would flesh them out in writing. But nothing. Why?

I was (and am) a perfectionist. I wanted to achieve my ideal - the Atlantics, the Brain Pickings - in one single step. Nothing I wrote ever felt good enough, looked the part, or sounded like the ideals I wanted.

Finding one’s voice can be a long journey.

Putting one’s thoughts out there on the interwebs does take quite a bit of courage.

But you can’t get any better until you put pen to paper, and take that first step.

Inspired by the approach of design thinking to Think, Make, Break, Repeat - here’s my first baby step. A collection of short essays / responses to books I’ve read, my personal projects, and anything I’m currently curious about.

Happy to stay in touch on LinkedIn or GitHub.
